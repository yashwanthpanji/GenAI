System Design Plan:
Architecture Dimension			What Good Looks Like
Scope clarity				Objective, boundaries, users, and non-goals are explicit and stable enough for design.
Input / output contract quality		Inputs, outputs, schemas, and review points are clearly defined and testable.
Workflow / orchestration design		Control flow is intentional, modular, observable, and easy to reason about.
Tool / integration boundary design	External systems are isolated behind clear contracts with manageable dependencies.
State, persistence, and traceability	Sessions, artifacts, decisions, and history are stored with auditability.
Human control model			Approvals, overrides, escalation, and review checkpoints are clearly defined.
Security and compliance posture		PII handling, secrets, access control, retention, and compliance risks are addressed.
Observability and diagnostics		Logs, metrics, traces, eventing, and debug paths make failures diagnosable.
Evaluation readiness			Success metrics, test harness, QA process, and acceptance criteria are measurable.
Maintainability and modularity		Services/components are reusable, versioned, and easy to change safely.
Scalability and performance readiness	The design can handle expected load and growth without major redesign.
Cost / latency balance			Model, infra, and integration choices are appropriate for target cost and responsiveness.
